  • Retention Using Vocabulary Journal and Metacognitive Writing Strategies (in press)
    Tasmia Mayen, Tauhid Tanjim, Sabrina Kaiser

    Abstract
    "…A quasi-experimental method has been applied to determine if targeted words can be learned using reflective writing on a vocabulary journal and show a higher retention rate in the long term. The result of the experiment, drawn from 27 participants, has shown that the strategic use of vocabulary journals as a tool is more effective than the traditional method of listing and memorizing meaning or definition. Results are discussed from theoretical and pedagogical perspectives."

    Journal of the Institute of Modern Languages, University of Dhaka, Volume 31, 2020 (In Press; expected publication date 31st December 2021)

  • Bangladeshi Celebrities on National Platforms: Code-Switching Variation and Sociolect
    Tasmia Mayen, Tauhid Tanjim, Mahmuda Akhter, Rakibul Hasan

    Abstract
    Sociolinguistic variants are indexical of social traits that are associated with identifiable speech communities. Unlike dialects, such distinguished variants are markers of every 'sociolect' that indicates the speaker's social class. In Bangladesh, the 'lower-class speech' variation is often associated with working-class people compared to the formal Bangla, which is considered as 'high-class speech' variation. However, the ‘popular’ vernacular falls in between the two extremes and are widely used in informal settings by educated middle and upper-middle-class speakers. Speaker uses different variants with cognitive awareness because it is a deterministic measure for speakers' prestige or social identity. After investigation, the gaps are identified based on two grounds. First, variation in Code-Switching (CS) in terms of probability of occurrences and patterns are found among different age groups of speakers belonging to the same social class and hence is a part of their sociolect. Second, the same speakers also use multiple variables from different sociolects, simultaneously varying on age. The paper examines cognitive sociolinguistic attitudes among famous Bengali celebrities who belong to the same social class to understand the correlation between independent variables (age, gender) and dependent variables (CS variation and phonological variables). The speakers are divided into three age groups, and the results indicate a strong statistical correlation with CS probability and patterns vs speakers’ age as well as non-standard variables vs age. However, a weaker correlation is found with CS probability vs speaker’s age and CS probability vs non-standard phonological variables. The results demonstrate the CS variation, variation in CS probability and use of a non-standard variant of verbs are higher among the younger upper/upper-middle class Bengali community than the older generation. While CS in this case represents overt prestige, popularizing non-standard verbs in various formal settings are an indicator of a linguistic market and a possible linguistic shift and pattern change in their sociolect. From this study, we can infer that CS is becoming part of the sociolect and possibly indicating a language shift that is becoming more apparent with the generation gap.

    (Sociolinguistics in Bangladesh: Voices of the unheard, 1st Edition, Edited Book, Routledge, (Project/book chapter, expected year of publication: 2022)

  • Finding the Safest Optimal Route Using Dijkstra's Shortest Path Algorithm with Application to Machine Learning, VGI and Real-time Crowdsourced Data: Building Prototype Model
    Tauhid Tanjim, Dr. Mosaddek Khan, Tasmia Mayen

    Abstract
    "…We propose Safer-Route Map Builder, a prototype crowdsourced digital pedestrian map construction system. The proposed system generates digital pedestrian maps for users using crowdsourcing, which will gradually collect, combine, and build up data over time and share it in real-time. The proposed prototype is thoroughly evaluated through simulation and tested implementation. We examine the viability of partnering with local developers to create a custom survey and motivate crowd contributors from their communities to give input on their experience or journey on a safety measurement scale. The results reveal that our proposed system can automatically construct digital pedestrian maps for detecting crime-prone areas at different hours of the day, risk-rating a route with high accuracy, and sending SOS notification for a person in distress cost of marginal delay."

    (under review; expected year of publication 2022)
